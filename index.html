About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), where I am working with Prof. Hanghang Tong. Prior to joining UIUC, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Hanyang University and I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Hanyang University under the supervision of Prof. Sang-Wook Kim. My research focus lies in a wide range of topics related to large-scale machine learning and data mining especially for real-world network analysis and recommender systems.

  • Large-Scale Machine Learning
    • Learning Rate Scheduling for Large-Batch DNN Training (WWW'22)
    • Data Parallelism for Distributed Machine Learning (IEEE IPDPS'21, ACM CIKM'21)
    • Quantization for Efficient Recommender Systems (IEEE ICDM'21)
  • Real-world Network Analysis
    • Efficient Graph Engine for Large-Scale Networks (ACM CIKM'22)
    • Influence Maximization in Social Networks (WWW'16, InfoSci'18)
